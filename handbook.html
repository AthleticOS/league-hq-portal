Mission, Vision & Values

The Bangkok International School Athletics League (BISAL) was established with the goal of fostering a love for sportsmanship and competition between schools[cite: 65, 69]. We achieve this by organizing sporting events that create a positive, mindful, and inclusive environment[cite: 70].

Membership Guidelines & Responsibilities

All schools involved in BISAL-organized events must share and support the league's Aims and Vision[cite: 92, 94].

  • Each school needs a designated point person, such as an Athletic Director or qualified PE Teacher[cite: 95].
  • Schools must have access to safe and appropriate facilities to host or co-host BISAL tournaments[cite: 96].
  • A coach or qualified adult supervisor must accompany each team to all fixtures[cite: 98].
  • Schools are expected to participate in at least three of the core sports across two or three age categories throughout the academic year[cite: 99].

Tournament Formats & Procedures

Tournaments will follow standardized bracket alignments to ensure uniform competition across the league[cite: 49, 450].

  • 3 to 5 Teams: Round Robin format[cite: 451, 452]. Each team plays each other in a league-style event[cite: 452]. No requirement for a final[cite: 452].
  • 6 Teams: 2 Pools of 3 teams[cite: 453, 455]. Cross-over semi-finals (1st Pool A vs 2nd Pool B)[cite: 456]. 3rd place in groups play each other at the same time as the final[cite: 457].
  • 8 Teams: 2 Pools of 4 teams[cite: 461, 463]. Cross-over semi-finals[cite: 464]. 3rd in groups play each other, and 4th vs 4th[cite: 465].

Respect & Code of Conduct

BISAL is committed to fostering a positive and respectful environment for everyone involved[cite: 44, 348, 350].

Coaches & Teachers [cite: 352]

Public criticism of officials is strictly prohibited[cite: 364]. Entering the field of play without the referee's permission is not allowed[cite: 365]. Violations may result in a one-year ban from coaching in BISAL events[cite: 370].

Players [cite: 371]

Players must follow the referee's instructions and avoid questioning decisions[cite: 391]. Cheating, complaining, or wasting time are unacceptable behaviors[cite: 380]. Sportsmanship includes shaking hands with opponents and officials after the game[cite: 392].

Spectators [cite: 399]

Schools are responsible for the conduct of their attending spectators[cite: 38, 280, 283]. Minor incidents will result in a warning to the school representative[cite: 284, 285]. Repeated offenses may result in team point deductions, spectator ejection, or disqualification from the event[cite: 287, 289, 290].

Health, Safety & Head Injury Protocol

Head Injury Action Plan [cite: 51, 52, 610]

  • Remove player from play immediately[cite: 589]. Rest 15-30 minutes and apply ice[cite: 572, 574, 582].
  • The Head of PE or Athletic Director must be informed regarding any symptomatic concussion cases[cite: 580].
  • Return to sport will be gradual and monitored via the sports department after receipt of a doctor's medical clearance letter[cite: 581].

Illness Policy [cite: 53, 620]

Students feeling unwell should avoid BISAL tournaments[cite: 622]. As a precaution, players with minor, non-contagious symptoms are permitted to wear masks while participating[cite: 624].
