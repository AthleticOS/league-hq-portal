League Handbook & Guidelines

Mission, Vision & Values

The Bangkok International School Athletics League (BISAL) was established with the goal of fostering a love for sportsmanship and competition between schools. We achieve this by organizing sporting events that create a positive, mindful, and inclusive environment.

Vision

Embrace Uniqueness, Achieve Greatness, Experience Success.

Membership Guidelines & Responsibilities

All schools involved in BISAL-organized events must share and support the league's Aims and Vision.

  • Each school needs a designated point person, such as an Athletic Director or qualified PE Teacher.
  • Schools must have access to safe and appropriate facilities to host or co-host BISAL tournaments.
  • A coach or qualified adult supervisor must accompany each team to all fixtures.
  • Schools are expected to participate in at least three of the core sports across two or three age categories throughout the academic year.

Tournament Formats & Procedures

Tournaments will follow standardized bracket alignments to ensure uniform competition across the league.

  • 3 to 5 Teams: Round Robin format. Each team plays each other in a league-style event. No requirement for a final.
  • 6 Teams: 2 Pools of 3 teams. Cross-over semi-finals (1st Pool A vs 2nd Pool B). 3rd place in groups play each other at the same time as the final.
  • 8 Teams: 2 Pools of 4 teams. Cross-over semi-finals. 3rd in groups play each other, and 4th vs 4th.

Agreed Tie-Breaker Formula

  1. Head to Head record
  2. Goal / Points difference
  3. Total Points scored

Respect & Code of Conduct

BISAL is committed to fostering a positive and respectful environment for everyone involved.

Coaches & Teachers

Public criticism of officials is strictly prohibited. Entering the field of play without the referee's permission is not allowed. Violations may result in a one-year ban from coaching in BISAL events.

Players

Players must follow the referee's instructions and avoid questioning decisions. Cheating, complaining, or wasting time are unacceptable behaviors. Sportsmanship includes shaking hands with opponents and officials after the game.

Spectators

Schools are responsible for the conduct of their attending spectators. Minor incidents will result in a warning to the school representative. Repeated offenses may result in team point deductions, spectator ejection, or disqualification from the event.

Health, Safety & Head Injury Protocol

Head Injury Action Plan

  • Remove player from play immediately. Rest 15-30 minutes and apply ice.
  • The Head of PE or Athletic Director must be informed regarding any symptomatic concussion cases.
  • Return to sport will be gradual and monitored via the sports department after receipt of a doctor's medical clearance letter.

Illness Policy

Students feeling unwell should avoid BISAL tournaments. As a precaution, players with minor, non-contagious symptoms are permitted to wear masks while participating.
